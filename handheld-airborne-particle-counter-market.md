<p><p>The handheld airborne particle counter market is categorized into ionizing and non-ionizing types based on their detection mechanisms. Ionizing counters utilize charged particles to identify and quantify airborne contaminants, effectively detecting smaller particles with high accuracy. In contrast, non-ionizing counters employ light scattering techniques, using lasers or LEDs to analyze particle size and concentration. Both types serve critical roles in industries like pharmaceuticals, cleanrooms, and environmental monitoring, ensuring compliance with air quality standards and safety regulations.</p></p>
